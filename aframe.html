A-Frame

Studying how Northwestern students study outside, Designing A-Frame.


Product Design


Winter 2017


A-frame is a multifunctional, lightweight chair made entirely out of recycled cardboard that allows students to relax and work outside. The frame is composed of “cardboard mache”: strips of used cardboard mixed with glue and water, and then set in a wooden mold. The seat is laser cut and attached sturdily through a slit in the frame.

I. CHALLENGE

It’s the springtime in Evanston, and Northwestern students are anxious to spend time outside. When floating between classes, they usually go to the student center, or the library, but on nice days, they’d rather work outside. Unfortunately, Deering Lawn, the open space most compatible with this need, offers no public seating. Moreover, sitting on the ground poses a range of problems: nowhere to put your laptop, damp grass, and nothing to lean against.


HOW MIGHT WE...use public seating to offer a useful study space for Northwestern students during springtime?

[Design Principles]
