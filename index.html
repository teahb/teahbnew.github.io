Teah Khadijeh Brands (b. 1996) is a spatial designer, researcher, and cultural worker living and making in Bedford-Stuyvesant, Brooklyn. Her fascination with culture (physical and digital, enduring and emerging) is at the root of her practice. She finds herself drawn to ‘intimate public spaces’: small-scale habitats that comfort, amuse, and are shared by community. Her personhood and methods are the product of Black culture, memes, academia, the tri-state area, and the 2000s.
